=====================================================================
  SIMULATION APPROACH AND TRADEOFFS
  Where the model matches reality, where it diverges, and why
=====================================================================

This document explains the design decisions, simplifications, and
tradeoffs made in this RBMK-1000 simulation. The goal is educational
accuracy — capturing the mechanisms and chain of causation — not
numerical precision. A real reactor physics code (MCNP, Serpent,
OpenMC) uses billions of neutron histories and coupled thermal-
hydraulics. We use thousands and a 50x20 grid.


=====================================================================
  1. NEUTRON TRANSPORT: AGENT-BASED vs. DETERMINISTIC
=====================================================================

  REALITY:
    A real RBMK core contains ~190 tonnes of UO2 fuel in 1,661
    pressure channels. At full power, the neutron population is
    on the order of 10^8 per cm^3. Neutron transport is governed
    by the Boltzmann transport equation, solved either by Monte
    Carlo methods (tracking individual particle histories through
    continuous-energy cross-section libraries) or by deterministic
    methods (discrete ordinates, diffusion theory).

  OUR MODEL:
    Agent-based Monte Carlo. Each neutron is an individual particle
    with position, velocity, and energy state (fast or thermal).
    Interactions are resolved probabilistically per cell traversal.

  WHAT MATCHES:
    - Neutrons are born fast (~2 MeV), must thermalize via
      moderator collisions to cause fission — correct.
    - Fission is probabilistic per traversal (macroscopic cross-
      section analogy) — conceptually correct.
    - Moderator scattering randomizes direction (isotropic) — a
      valid simplification of the ~115 real collisions needed.
    - Fast neutrons pass through absorption rods — correct (the
      boron-10 absorption cross-section is thermal-dominated).
    - Neutrons bounce off core boundaries — models the graphite
      reflector surrounding the real core.

  WHAT DIVERGES:
    - We model TWO energy groups (fast/thermal). Real transport
      codes use continuous energy or 100+ groups. The thermalization
      is instant on moderator contact rather than gradual.
    - Our 50x20 grid (~1,000 cells) is vastly simplified. The real
      core has 1,661 fuel channels in a cylindrical geometry.
    - We cap neutrons at 5,000 particles for performance. Real
      codes track millions of histories.
    - Fission probability is a constant (FISSION_PROBABILITY = 0.20
      per cell traversal). In reality it depends on energy, fuel
      temperature (Doppler broadening), burnup, etc.
    - We use weighted particles (rayon-parallelized): one particle
      can represent multiple physical neutrons. This reduces
      allocation overhead but increases statistical variance.

  WHY THIS TRADEOFF:
    The agent-based approach makes the physics VISIBLE. You can
    watch individual neutrons scatter, thermalize, get absorbed,
    and cause fission. This is impossible with a point-kinetics
    model (differential equations) that treats the core as a
    single lumped parameter.


=====================================================================
  2. TIME COMPRESSION
=====================================================================

  REALITY:
    - I-135 half-life: 6.57 hours
    - Xe-135 half-life: 9.17 hours
    - Xe-135 peaks ~11 hours after shutdown ("iodine pit")
    - The 9-hour delay between initial power reduction and test
      execution was critical for xenon accumulation.
    - The actual accident sequence from 01:23:04 (test start) to
      01:23:47 (explosion) was 43 SECONDS.

  OUR MODEL:
    - I-135 decay rate compressed from 6.57h to ~5s (lambda=0.14)
    - Xe-135 decay compressed from 9.17h to 45s
    - The 24-hour real timeline is compressed to ~200 seconds
    - The 43-second test-to-explosion sequence spans ~40 sim seconds

  WHAT MATCHES:
    - The RELATIVE dynamics are preserved: iodine decays into
      xenon, xenon poisons the reactor, xenon burns off when
      flux returns. The "iodine pit" mechanism works correctly.
    - The compressed timescale still shows the causal sequence:
      power drop → xenon buildup → rod withdrawal → coolant loss
      → void coefficient → positive scram → destruction.

  WHAT DIVERGES:
    - In reality, the xenon pit deepens over HOURS, not seconds.
      Operators had time to discuss, argue, and make phone calls.
      Our simulation compresses this deliberation into seconds.
    - The water heating cycle (cool → warm → vapor) takes seconds
      in the sim vs. minutes in reality, making the void
      coefficient feedback appear faster than it was.
    - The simulation speed multiplier (1x-4x) further compresses
      time, which can make some effects appear instantaneous.

  WHY THIS TRADEOFF:
    A real-time simulation of the xenon pit would require the user
    to watch for 11+ hours. Compression preserves causation while
    making the physics observable in a few minutes.


=====================================================================
  3. DELAYED NEUTRONS
=====================================================================

  REALITY:
    6 precursor groups with half-lives from 0.2s to 55s. For fresh
    U-235 fuel, beta-eff is ~0.0064. However, by the time of the
    Chernobyl accident, significant fuel burnup had produced fissile
    Pu-239 and Pu-241, which have much lower delayed fractions
    (~0.21%). The effective beta for the mixed fuel was ~0.005.
    These delayed neutrons are what makes a reactor controllable —
    without them, the generation time would be ~10^-4 seconds
    (uncontrollable).

  OUR MODEL:
    One effective precursor group: beta = 0.005, lambda = 0.08/s.
    Fission deposits a fraction of its yield into a precursor pool
    that decays into neutrons over time.

  WHAT MATCHES:
    - The delayed fraction (0.50%) matches the burned-fuel RBMK
      value at the time of the accident.
    - The concept that delayed neutrons make control possible is
      correctly demonstrated — at subcritical/critical states, the
      reactor responds on a seconds timescale, not microseconds.
    - When reactivity exceeds beta (prompt supercritical), delayed
      neutrons become irrelevant and the reactor period drops to
      milliseconds — correctly modeled.

  WHAT DIVERGES:
    - Six precursor groups compressed to one. The real decay
      spectrum has components ranging from 0.2s to 55s. Our single
      group (tau ≈ 12.5s) captures the average but not the spread.
    - Delayed neutrons spawn at random fuel cells, not at their
      original fission site. In reality, precursors are embedded
      in the fuel where fission occurred.


=====================================================================
  4. CONTROL ROD MECHANICS
=====================================================================

  REALITY:
    - 211 control rods total (various groups: manual, shortened
      absorber, automatic regulators).
    - Full insertion time: 18-21 seconds (0.4 m/s).
    - Control rod channels had separate low-pressure cooling
      circuits — this water did NOT boil during the accident.
    - The graphite displacer is 4.5m long with a 1.25m water
      column at each end when fully withdrawn.
    - During SCRAM, rods seized at 2-2.5m of 7m travel due to
      fuel channel deformation from the power excursion —
      ruptured fuel, swollen pressure tubes, and shifted graphite
      blocks physically buckled the control rod channels.

  OUR MODEL:
    - 5 control rods (one per 10-column zone).
    - SCRAM speed: 1.5 rows/sec (~13s for full insertion).
    - Graphite displacer tip: bottom 2 rows of each rod, with
      a 1.5x reactivity boost during insertion.
    - Channel deformation modeled as power-threshold resistance:
      when per-zone activation rate exceeds a threshold (major
      power excursion), resistance increases proportionally,
      slowing and eventually stopping rod insertion.
    - Rods insert freely at first during SCRAM, then jam once
      the displacer-tip-induced power excursion deforms channels.

  WHAT MATCHES:
    - The "positive scram" mechanism: graphite tips increase
      local reactivity before the boron absorber arrives. This
      is the key Chernobyl mechanism and is correctly modeled.
    - Gradual rod insertion (not instant) with realistic speed.
    - Channel deformation from the power excursion jams rods at
      a depth consistent with the historical ~2-2.5m of 7m.
    - Rod channels move freely during normal operation (separate
      low-pressure cooling, no steam resistance).
    - The displacer tips are visually distinct (blue vs. red)
      so the user can see them leading the rod into the core.

  WHAT DIVERGES:
    - 5 rods vs. 211 real rods. We cannot model individual rod
      patterns, partial withdrawals, or the complex spatial
      distribution of rods across the real 1,661-channel core.
    - The 1.25m water column gap is abstracted as a 2-row boost
      zone rather than physically modeled as a water displacement.
    - Channel deformation is modeled as a function of power level
      rather than explicit fuel mechanics (fragmentation, swelling,
      tube rupture). The result is correct but the mechanism is
      simplified.
    - Auto-control uses a simple proportional controller with
      dead-band, not the real LAR/AR-1/AR-2 automatic regulators
      which had their own complex (and buggy) behavior.
    - The scenario uses target-based rod movement (gradual),
      while the real rods had individual servo drives with
      independent failure modes.


=====================================================================
  5. WATER / COOLANT MODEL
=====================================================================

  REALITY:
    - Light water at ~7 MPa (280°C) flows through 1,661 individual
      pressure tubes at ~24,000 m^3/hour total.
    - Each channel has its own flow rate, temperature profile, and
      void fraction.
    - Boiling occurs in the upper portion of the channel (two-phase
      flow). The void fraction varies axially and radially.
    - At Chernobyl, the MCP (main circulation pump) flow was
      measured at ~56,000-58,000 m^3/hour (abnormally high due to
      extra pumps) before the test.

  OUR MODEL:
    - Three water states per cell: Cool, Warm, Vapor.
    - Neutron hits accumulate; at thresholds, water transitions.
    - Vapor returns to Warm→Cool over time scaled by coolant flow.
    - Global coolant flow multiplier (0.0-2.0) controls cooling.
    - Pressure derived from global void fraction / coolant flow.

  WHAT MATCHES:
    - The positive void coefficient: steam cells have no neutron
      absorption, increasing local fission probability by 1.3x.
      This drives the runaway feedback loop correctly.
    - Reduced coolant flow → slower vapor condensation → more
      steam accumulates → more positive reactivity.
    - The visual progression (blue → red → black) makes the
      void formation visible and intuitive.

  WHAT DIVERGES:
    - Water state is per-cell, not per-channel. Real channels
      have axial void profiles; our model has uniform states.
    - Three discrete states vs. continuous void fraction. Real
      boiling is a gradual phase transition.
    - No two-phase flow dynamics, no slip ratio, no subcooled
      boiling, no departure from nucleate boiling (DNB).
    - Pressure is a global derived quantity, not a per-channel
      measurement with steam drum dynamics.
    - The 1.3x void coefficient boost is a simplification of a
      complex function of void fraction, enrichment, burnup,
      and rod configuration.


=====================================================================
  6. THE PARALLEL ARCHITECTURE
=====================================================================

  REALITY (computational):
    Real Monte Carlo transport codes (MCNP, Serpent, OpenMC) run
    millions to billions of neutron histories on HPC clusters.
    Each history is independent — embarrassingly parallel.

  OUR MODEL:
    Rayon-parallelized neutron transport with a two-phase approach:
    Phase 1 (parallel): process all neutrons against a read-only
    grid snapshot. Phase 2 (sequential): apply grid mutations.

  WHAT MATCHES:
    - Neutron histories are independent — each rayon worker
      processes its assigned neutrons without synchronization.
    - Thread-local RNG (rand::thread_rng) prevents contention,
      matching real Monte Carlo parallel implementations.

  WHAT DIVERGES:
    - The two-phase approach means all neutrons in a frame see
      the same grid state (beginning-of-frame snapshot). In the
      sequential version, early neutrons' effects were visible to
      later neutrons within the same frame.
    - "Phantom fission" filtering: if two neutrons fission the
      same cell in one frame, the second is discarded. This is
      an artifact of the parallel approach that doesn't exist in
      sequential processing.
    - Weighted particles (one particle representing multiple
      neutrons) are used to reduce allocation pressure. The weight
      infrastructure is present but particles split aggressively
      (WEIGHT_SPLIT_THRESHOLD = 2.0) to maintain spatial diversity.
    - Grid timer updates (iodine decay, water cooling) are also
      parallelized by row with rayon.


=====================================================================
  7. WHAT THE SIMULATION GETS RIGHT (SUMMARY)
=====================================================================

  The following causal mechanisms are physically correct and emerge
  naturally from the simulation's rules — they are not scripted:

  1. Chain reaction requires thermalized neutrons (graphite moderator)
  2. Xenon-135 poisons the reactor after power reduction (iodine pit)
  3. Rod withdrawal is necessary to overcome xenon
  4. Loss of coolant flow → steam → positive void coefficient → surge
  5. SCRAM with graphite tips → initial reactivity INCREASE
  6. Power excursion deforms channels → rods jam at ~30% depth
  7. The disaster emerges as a natural consequence of the RBMK's
     design flaws when combined with the operator actions

  The Chernobyl disaster is not "played back" — it is RECREATED
  by the physics engine given the same sequence of inputs. Change
  the inputs (don't withdraw all the rods, don't reduce coolant
  flow, use a reactor without positive void coefficient) and the
  outcome changes.


=====================================================================
  8. WHAT THE SIMULATION GETS WRONG (SUMMARY)
=====================================================================

  1. Scale: 5 rods / 1,000 cells vs. 211 rods / 1,661 channels
  2. Energy groups: 2 (fast/thermal) vs. continuous energy
  3. Time compression: hours → seconds for xenon dynamics
  4. Water model: 3 discrete states vs. continuous two-phase flow
  5. Geometry: 2D rectangular grid vs. 3D cylindrical core
  6. No fuel temperature feedback (Doppler broadening)
  7. No structural mechanics (fuel swelling, channel deformation)
  8. No radiation transport (gamma heating, decay heat)
  9. No containment model (the RBMK had minimal containment)
  10. No atmospheric dispersion (the 50-day fire, the plume)

  These omissions are deliberate. Each would add complexity without
  improving the educational value of watching neutrons, rods, water,
  and xenon interact in real time.
